  • IO Molecule

    IO Molecule is a simple breakout board for exploring interaction & experience design. It's basically a breakout board for the Adafruit Feather platform with JST-SH connectors and some onboard components.

    • Setup

      Download the Arduino IDE
      Follow the steps in this tutorial to install the Adafruit Boards in the IDE
      Select the Adafruit Bluefruit Micro board from Tools > Board
      Select the correct serial port from Tools > Port
      Open the Blink Example from Files > Examples > 01.Basics > Blink
      Press the 'upload' icon on the top left and see if it works.
      Now download or copy this template project into a sketch and get going!
      If you don't have the Neopixel Library, go to Sketch > Include Library > Manage Libraries and install the Adafruit NeoPixel Library.

    • Pinout

      You can read which Arduino pins belong to which connector on the template file.
      For more details, check out the (somewhat messy) schematic.

    • Inputs

      There are 3 different kind of connectors with 2, 3 & 4 wires each. The black wire is always the ground connection and index 0.
      Button: solder the two wires diagonally.
      Potentiometer: 0 - Left, 1 - Middle, 2 - Right

  • Hyper Island - Experience Prototyping (Nov 2018)

    A two-day workshop for the Hyper Island Digital Media program that explores the physical digital continuum.

    • Video Summary

      Sieh dir diesen Beitrag auf Instagram an

      Ein Beitrag geteilt von felix makes (@felixmakes_) am Nov 27, 2018 um 10:11 PST

    • Brief 1: Create a signal that travels from digital to physical

      Focus on creating something that has very low friction between the digital input and the physical output.

    • Brief 2: Create a signal that travels from physical to digital.

      Make an input device. Explore the relationship between interaction with the input device and the action it triggers on the computer. Explore the affordances for your input. Think about these questions: What do you think you could do with it? What gestures do you do intuitively? What cultural references come to mind?

      To get started, go to Sketch > Include Library > Manage Libraries and install the 'HID-Project' Library.
      Make a new sketch and copy this HID molecule template code into it.
      For more info about HID commands, you can check the HID-project documentation.
      You will probably have reselect the port after every time you upload since the arduino will reboot as an HID device.

      Overall, we recommend to test your inputs without the HID functionality first, as it can be quite tricky to fix bugs :)

    • Brief 3: Create a game that spans both digital and physical.

      You decide what components are physical and which ones are digital. Try to think outside the normal 'sitting still and using your hands' type of games.

  • Malmö University - Experiene Prototyping (SS 2018)

    Interactive Product Design is about how we use, experience and understand technological products. In this course we will be exploring how we can use design and engineering to explore interactive products.

    • Introduction

      Designers of interactive products create behaviours that link the digital with the physical. We define how actions and events are organised and sequenced over time. We also design how these products communicate to people. In this course you will gain a basic knowledge of materials, approaches and methods for Interactive Product Design. You will begin to comprehend the complexity of interactive systems, and to be able to design and prototype simplified, logical, satisfying, even delightful experiences. You will be able to communicate and document your interactive design work through sketching, prototyping, and presentations.

    • Brief 2: Design a single purpose input device

      Design an input device made for a special purpose. Explore the relationship between interaction with the input device and the action it triggers on the computer. Start with the sensors you have, but also explore other sensors that might work for your interaction and make prototypes.

      Look at the affordances of materials and shapes. When you evaluate your ideas, think about some of these questions: When you look at it, what do you think you could do with it? When you manipulate it, what gestures do you do intuitively? When you think of it, what cultural references come to mind?

      Use your newly learned electronics and Arduino skills to wire up the sensor(s) you need for your input device and use the template we provided to trigger keyboard actions. There are different keyboard shortcuts for Mac and Windows. Most apps also have their own shortcuts.
      Remember, make and document lots of quick mockups, do iterations. Don’t make a polished design first, but test out your interactions using low-fi prototypes. Make sure to pick an idea in time so you can still make one somewhat polished prototype you can demo in class.

      Have the prototype ready to demo for the Seminar on Friday, April 27th and make sure to send us a link to your documentation on Tumblr before that. Please include at least one gif of the final product that we can share on the course website.
